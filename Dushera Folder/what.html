What people do on Dussehra

On Dussehra, Hindus perform puja (worship) to mark the victory of good over evil. In many parts of India, people participate in processions and cultural events, and there are often large effigies of the demon king, Ravana, that are burnt to symbolize the victory of good over evil. Some people also observe fasts or perform special rituals on this day.

Dussehra is also a time for family and friends to come together and celebrate. Many people visit temples and participate in puja ceremonies, and there are often food stalls and other festivities in public places. In some parts of India, people also visit the homes of relatives and friends to exchange gifts and greetings.
