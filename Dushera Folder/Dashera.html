Celebrating Dussehra

Dussehra is a popular festival celebrated in many parts of India and around the world. It is a celebration of the victory of good over evil and the triumph of righteousness. This festival is marked by a variety of cultural and religious traditions, including the performance of plays, the lighting of lamps, and the breaking of clay pots. In this article, we will explore the significance of Dussehra and how it is celebrated.

What do you do on Dussehra?
  • Performing plays and dramas that depict the story of Lord Rama and his victory over Ravana
  • Lighting lamps or candles to symbolize the triumph of good over evil
  • Breaking clay pots or participating in other fun and festive activities
  • Attending religious ceremonies and prayers at temples and other holy places
  • Exchanging gifts and sweets with friends and family

No matter how you choose to celebrate Dussehra, it is a time to come together with loved ones and celebrate the victory of good over evil. It is a time of joy, celebration, and reflection on the important lessons of the Hindu tradition.
